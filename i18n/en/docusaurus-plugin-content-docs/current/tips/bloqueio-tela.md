# <Icon name="Unlock" size="lg" /> Disable Screen Lock

Step-by-step guide to disable screen lock on Android and avoid losing connection with the Z-API.

---

## Introduction

As you may have already noticed, when your phone locks its screen, WhatsApp stops running in the background, which can cause a loss of internet connection, leading to an interruption of your WhatsApp Web connection. Consequently, if you are connected to the Z-API, your service might be affected. That's why we've provided a step-by-step guide to mitigate this issue.

**The examples below were performed on an Android phone:**

---

## First Step

Access the Android settings.

![Android Settings - Step 1](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio1.jpeg)

---

## Second Step

Then, go to "Screen lock and passwords".

![Screen Lock - Step 2](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio2.jpeg)

---

## Third Step

Select the always active option.

![Always Active - Step 3](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio3.jpeg)

---

## Fourth Step

Next, go to Passwords and Security.

![Passwords and Security - Step 4](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio4.jpeg)

---

## Fifth Step

Choose the passwords option.

![Passwords Option - Step 5](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio5.jpeg)

---

## Sixth Step

Disable the screen lock.

![Disable Screen Lock - Step 6](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio6.jpeg)

![Disable Screen Lock - Step 6.1](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio7.jpeg)

---

## Seventh Step

Congratulations! Your device will no longer automatically lock its screen, maintaining a stable connection with the Z-API.

![Screen Lock Disabled - Step 7](/img/WABRetirandoBloqueio8.jpeg)

---

## Important

- Disabling screen lock reduces device security
- Recommended only for dedicated devices
- Use in conjunction with "Always On Android" for better results
