## Introduction

Which is the better alternative for you?

To illustrate the use of a WhatsApp API: When WhatsApp initially decided to be part of its strategic choices, it chose to keep its API closed, quite different from what happened with other global players in the segment. That means developers did not have the opportunity to integrate their solutions and software technologies into WhatsApp.

Especially in this scenario, we are talking about companies with various market solutions, such as: CRM and ERP systems, creation of popular chatbots, and even e-commerce or retail businesses for real-time notification services on a customer's order status. During this period, many developers created or sought custom gateways that could provide access to the WhatsApp API. These solutions can still be found on GitHub. The problem with using these libraries is that WhatsApp makes updates which render the solutions outdated and, as a rule, when using these libraries, numbers were immediately banned.

## Official WhatsApp Business API

In 2017, WhatsApp opened access to its official API. Initially, the official WhatsApp API was tested behind closed doors and only some global companies could pay for it. But over time, more and more organizations gained access to the official WhatsApp API.

The cooperation order is such that, to connect to the official API, you will need to coordinate the connection with WhatsApp's official partners. That means you will have to work with intermediaries designed to provide an API and moderate your activities under current WhatsApp regulations. This implies a large number of restrictions, which will be shown in the table at the bottom. You can check the list of current official partners here: [https://www.facebook.com/business/partner-directory/search?solution_type=messaging](https://www.facebook.com/business/partner-directory/search?solution_type=messaging). This is very important because now some companies are misleading their customers, calling themselves official WhatsApp partners.

## Disadvantages of the Official API

- **Price**: The subscription fee for the official API can range from a few hundred dollars to thousands of dollars. Not to mention the costs of connecting and individual message fees on WhatsApp. Additionally, the price of these fees is dictated by WhatsApp (in current currency), it's not always possible to negotiate with an intermediary a more personalized approach. You can read more about prices on the official developer site: [https://developers.facebook.com/docs/whatsapp/pricing#usdusd](https://developers.facebook.com/docs/whatsapp/pricing#usdusd)

- **Minimum Volume**: Similarly, those who wish to connect to the official API must have a minimum message volume of 15,000 monthly, which is not a feasible condition for all companies.

- **Sector Restrictions**: WhatsApp does not approve partnerships with certain sectors. For example, finance and real estate are interesting for WhatsApp. And let's say businesses that sell vitamins or medications do not get approval from the messenger.

- **Bureaucratic Process**: Working with an official WhatsApp representative and obtaining the API will also require a lot of energy on your part. Collecting and processing documents, approving inspections, developing and approving models - all this can take a significant amount of time and end in failure. Unfortunately, this risk also exists.

- **Dependence on Mobile**
- **General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR)**
- **Delayed Updates**
- **Suspension and Blocking of Numbers**

## Being Banned by WhatsApp?

According to some statistics from WhatsApp, the messenger blocks around 2 million users per month who violate the terms of the WhatsApp policy.

But this does not mean that unofficial APIs are irresponsible with their customers. For example, Z-API for user security provides individual IPs to clients, performs secure authorization and inserts a system of artificial protectors, such as a queue to send messages to avoid mass sending and protect them from being banned.

There also exist systems to emulate a phone on a computer so that the work does not depend on a physical device. Of course, these methods will not help if the user neglects the rules for working with WhatsApp clients and spam. Even with the official API, it is possible to be banned by violating the WhatsApp policies. But if you combine the security tools of Z-API with usage within the recommendations of WhatsApp.
