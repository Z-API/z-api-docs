# <Icon name="Battery" size="lg" /> Always Active Android

Step-by-step guide to keep your Android device always active and ensure a stable connection with Z-API.

---

## Introduction

As every good dev knows, how much Apple makes our lives difficult, right? Unfortunately, we don't have a tutorial on how to keep iOS always active. If you discover how to do this for iOS, don't forget to let us know; we would be happy to update this topic.

Back to the Android topic, below we list 3 steps to help you keep your Android device always active and maintain a stable connection with Z-API.

---

## First Step

You will need to enable Developer Mode. To do this, access your phone's settings, then go to System and then About the device. Tap on the Build number 7 times in succession, and you're done.

![Developer Mode - Step 1](/img/WABAndroidAtivo.png)

---

## Second Step

After enabling Developer options, you will receive a prompt to confirm your screen unlock method. When you return to the previous menu, you will notice that additional options are available at the end of the menu.

![Developer Mode - Step 2](/img/WABAndroidAtivo1.png)

---

## Third Step

Now that we have Developer options, choose the option **Keep Screen Always On**. With this option enabled, your device will remain with the screen on even during battery charging.

![Keep Screen Always On](/img/WABAndroidAtivo2.png)

---

## Important

- This setting may consume more battery
- Recommended to use with device connected to charger
- Ideal for dedicated devices or emulators
