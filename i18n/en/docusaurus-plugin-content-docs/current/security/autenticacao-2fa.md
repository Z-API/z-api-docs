# Two-Factor Authentication

### Two-Factor Authentication Security

Implementing an additional authentication step, known as two-factor authentication (2FA), in the Z-API panel is an essential security measure to protect your information and instances against unauthorized access. This extra layer of protection requires you to provide a second factor of authentication in addition to your normal password when logging in.

### Enabling the Feature

To enable two-factor authentication in the Z-API panel, follow these steps:

1. Access the Z-API panel and log in with your existing credentials.
2. Navigate to the "Security" section.
3. Locate the "Two-Factor Authentication" option and click on "Configure Now".

![2FA Configuration Screen](https://developer.z-api.io/assets/images/two-factor-c16058ec223e2d733594c3e5a813728e.jpeg)

After clicking "Configure Now", the system will generate a unique QR code. This QR code should be synchronized with an authenticator app, such as 1Password, Google Authenticator, or Microsoft Authenticator, on your mobile device. The authenticator app will be used as the second factor of authentication.

### How It Works

1. Open the authenticator app on your mobile device.
2. Add a new account manually or scan the QR code generated by the Z-API panel.
3. The authenticator app will link your Z-API account to your device.
4. Now, each time you log in to the Z-API panel, you will be prompted to provide a one-time use code generated by the authenticator app.
5. This one-time use code is generated every few seconds and is unique to your account, meaning no one else can access your account even if they know your password.

### Benefits of Using 2FA

Implementing two-factor authentication brings several benefits for the security of your Z-API account:

1. **Additional Protection**: Even if someone knows your password, they cannot access your account without the second factor of authentication.
2. **Prevention of Unauthorized Access**: Two-Factor Authentication significantly hinders unauthorized access attempts because it requires an additional element that only the account holder possesses (your mobile device).
3. **Sensitive Data Security**: If you are handling confidential or critical information, two-factor authentication is a fundamental measure to ensure the integrity of these data.
4. **Compliance with Security Standards**: In many industries and regulations, two-factor authentication is a requirement to meet security standards.

Two-Factor Authentication is a vital layer of security that protects your accounts and information in the Z-API panel against cyber threats, making unauthorized access much more difficult and ensuring the integrity of your data. It is highly recommended to activate this functionality to enhance the security of your operations.
